01 Ownership

How we define ownership

Not about titles. About how you show up when something is broken, unclear, or off track. Speak up early, articulate the problem clearly, and bring people together to solve it. The higher the level, the less you're flagging and the more you're fixing

LVL 01Problem Dump • not acceptable"There's a problem."
LVL 02Problem Noted • baseline"There's a problem, and I think I know why."
LVL 03Problem Solver • all team members"Here's what's wrong and a few ways we could fix it."
LVL 04Solution Driven • team leads"Here's the problem, cause, options, and what I recommend."
LVL 05 ★Full Ownership • exec team"I found it, fixed it, and here's what we learned."

This framework is one part of how we think about performance — not the whole picture. Someone can be an exceptional specialist or collaborator at Level 2 or 3. What matters is that problems surface clearly and get routed to the right person to solve them

03 Communication

Meeting hygiene

Every meeting needs a reason to exist. If you called it, you own it. Fewer, sharper meetings beat more frequent, aimless ones every time

CHECKLIST · EVERY MEETING
Agenda sent 24h before
The organizer shares a short agenda the day before. Three bullet points max. No agenda, no meeting.
Minimum attendees
Invite only people who need to contribute or decide. Everyone else gets a summary afterward.
State the purpose first
Open with one sentence: are we informing, deciding, or exploring? Say it out loud. Pick one.
Named output at the end
Close with a named action and owner. No owner means no real action item.
Audit your recurring list
Team leads: review your own recurring meetings. Kill duplicates. 1:1s are exempt from public agenda policy.
It's okay to leave
If you've been in a meeting for 10 minutes and haven't contributed, leave. It's not rude — it means you're busy and have work to do. Manage your time.
↓
Fewer is better
Before booking, ask if this could be a Slack message, a Loom, or a shared doc. Async first.
→
One clear ask per meeting
A meeting that tries to do three things does none of them well. Pick the highest-priority outcome and build around it.
✓
Always write it down
If the outcome isn't captured after the call, it didn't happen. One person takes notes, every time.

04 Transparency

Build in the open

THE GOLDEN STANDARD
Anyone, at any time, should be able to open a Slack channel and know exactly what's happening.
No digging. No chasing people down. No privileged information locked in DMs. Every department channel should tell the full story of what the team is working on, what's blocked, and what just shipped. That's the bar we're building toward.
15%
SEPT 1 GOAL
TARGET: PUBLIC CHANNEL MESSAGES
We are working toward 15% of all Slack messages happening in public channels. Every team lead is accountable for modeling this.
THE RULE
Default to public
If a conversation doesn't need to be private, it shouldn't be in a DM. Move threads to shared channels. Managers: model this visibly.
WHY IT MATTERS
Visibility creates momentum
When wins and blocks are public, others can step in, celebrate, or help. Silence breeds a false sense of stillness. Post the update.

05 AI + Tools

We are an AI-first company

We don't just build AI products — we run on AI. Every person at Sapien is expected to use these tools daily, stay curious about what's new, and share what works with the team

THE EXPECTATION
If a task can be done faster or better with AI, use AI.
We are not a company that tolerates doing things slowly out of habit. Every person here should be leveraging AI tools to do the work of three people. That is not a figure of speech — it is a genuine operational expectation. Learn the tools. Share what works. Raise the bar constantly.
PRINCIPLE 01
Default to AI first
Before spending hours on something, ask: can AI draft, structure, summarize, or solve 80% of this in minutes? If yes, start there and refine
PRINCIPLE 02
Share what works
If you find a prompt, workflow, or tool that dramatically speeds up your work, post it publicly. One person's discovery becomes everyone's advantage
PRINCIPLE 03
Stay on the frontier
The AI landscape moves fast. Make it your job to know what the best tools are right now — not six months ago. Curiosity is a job requirement here
PRINCIPLE 04
Automate the repetitive
If you find yourself doing the same task more than twice, that's a signal. Build a workflow, use an agent, or ask if it can be automated. Manual loops are waste
PRINCIPLE 05
Human judgment on top
AI handles volume and speed. You bring context, taste, and accountability. The output is yours — own it fully, regardless of how it was generated
PRINCIPLE 06
Access is not a barrier
If you need a tool and don't have access, ask. We will get it for you. No one should be working without the right instruments

06 Build in Community

Build in community

Building in the open internally is step one. The next level is bringing the world along with us — builders, contributors, and believers who are part of what we're creating

THE VISION
The best companies don't just ship in public — they build with their community in real time.
Building in community means our contributors, developers, and token holders aren't just end users — they're part of the process. They see what we're working on. They give feedback before it ships. They celebrate wins alongside us. This is how trust gets built at scale, and how a protocol becomes something people genuinely care about.
INTERNAL
Build in the open
Within Sapien, default to public Slack channels. Anyone should be able to see what every team is working on at any moment. No hidden silos
EXTERNAL
Build in community
With our contributors and the world: share progress publicly, narrate the journey, invite people in. Real-time updates beat polished retrospectives
HOW WE COMMUNICATE
Relentlessly positive
We work in a technology that could transform humanity for the better. We talk about what's possible, what's exciting, and what we're building toward. We stay in our lane. We don't punch at competitors or wade into negativity
THE STANDARD
"Don't summarize the past. Narrate the present."
The most magnetic companies publish what they're learning right now — the experiments, the pivots, the discoveries. That's what builds a real community around a product
💬
Show your work
Post Looms. Share prototypes. Drop screenshots of things in progress. The more people see being built, the more invested they become
📈
Celebrate publicly
When something ships, when a milestone is hit, when a contributor does great work — say it out loud and in public. Wins shared are wins multiplied
🌍
Bring contributors in
Our network of contributors isn't just a labour pool — they're the heartbeat of the protocol. Treat them like co-builders, because that's exactly what they are
